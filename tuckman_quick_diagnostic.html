Every team goes through predictable stages of development. Knowing where you are helps you work more effectively — and move forward with intention. This tool is grounded in Tuckman's foundational research on group development.

Help

This tool helps student team members figure out where their team stands in the Tuckman cycle of group development — and what to do about it. Built for university consulting and project courses.

You'll get a diagnosed stage, a confidence score, and concrete next steps. The Detailed Diagnostic also breaks your results down by behavior category.

ⓘ

Important: Results reflect one team member's perception. Compare with teammates — disagreements are useful data.

Tuckman (1965); Tuckman & Jensen (1977). Note: progression is not always linear — teams can and do regress. Not all teams reach Performing.

⚡ Quick Diagnostic

Read each statement. Check all that describe your team right now. You'll get a stage diagnosis in about 2 minutes.

🔍 Detailed Diagnostic

For each criterion, pick Present / Somewhat / Not Present based on what you actually observe — not what you wish were true. Work through all five stages. You'll get a confidence score and a category breakdown.

What does "Somewhat" mean?

For "Communication is more frequent, open, and collaborative than in previous stages" — pick Somewhat if you're seeing improvement but it's not consistent yet. Present means you see it clearly and reliably. Not Present means there's no real evidence of it.

💡

Tip: Take the diagnostic on your own first, then compare with your teammates. Where you disagree is usually the most useful part.
